# Human-Centered Portfolio Redesign

## Status

This branch is a design exploration. It must not be merged or deployed until the interactive desktop and mobile result is visually approved by Mario.

The first executable prototype now includes the replacement homepage, real screenshots of public work, open-license visualizations, the historical provenance photograph, an evidence-led model-activity graphic and a privacy-safe focus-group protocol visual. The next gate is human inspection of the rendered desktop and mobile result—not merely successful compilation.

## Why the previous direction is rejected

The current portfolio is mechanically valid but visually synthetic. Its oversized gradient headline, abstract evidence-network diagram, pastel role cards, uniform rounded surfaces, dense explanatory copy and blanket reveal animations make it resemble an AI-generated SaaS landing page. The result is harder to read, less memorable and less human than the older portfolio.

The redesign must retain factual accuracy, specialized CVs, accessibility, raw-HTML content, deterministic builds and evidence boundaries while replacing the public visual and editorial system.

## Design principles

1. **Real work before abstract positioning.** Use actual project screenshots, published visualizations, public records and open-license photographs.
2. **Human reading rhythm.** Prefer short paragraphs, clear subheads, generous but purposeful space and a comfortable text measure.
3. **Editorial character.** Use a warm paper-like palette, strong typography, visible rules, asymmetric compositions and restrained shapes instead of glassmorphism or generic bento cards.
4. **Professional breadth without career-theory overload.** The homepage should show what Mario does and has produced. Role families and specialized CVs belong near the end, after evidence.
5. **Purposeful motion.** Animate image crops, section rules, captions and selected interactions. Do not fade every section into view or hide content before scrolling.
6. **Progressive disclosure without information loss.** Keep detailed evidence accessible through case pages, master CV and expandable sections.
7. **Mobile is a designed experience.** Do not simply stack desktop cards into a very long column.
8. **No fake proof.** Do not generate synthetic product imagery, fabricated testimonials or decorative security dashboards.

## Proposed visual language

- Warm ivory and paper neutrals.
- Deep ink/navy for text.
- Cobalt, vermilion and muted moss as controlled accents.
- Editorial serif display type paired with a highly readable sans-serif body.
- Mostly rectangular compositions with modest radii, visible borders and offset print-like shadows.
- Real media with captions and clear provenance.
- Light texture rather than glowing gradient backgrounds.

## Proposed homepage structure

1. Direct hero with a short umbrella statement and a collage of real artifacts.
2. Three capabilities in plain language: verify evidence, organize research, test systems.
3. Four visual case stories with role, process and result.
4. A public-artifact gallery using real visualizations, product views and records.
5. Experience narrative, including sensitive focus-group research collaboration.
6. Compact role-fit and specialized-CV selector.
7. Contact and primary verification links.

## Image and privacy policy

Public product pages may be captured as static screenshots. Wikimedia assets must retain attribution and license links. Personal photographs, private correspondence, focus-group materials, participant information and unpublished research documents must not be shown without explicit permission.

## Release gates

- Human visual review at desktop and mobile sizes.
- No essential content hidden by animation.
- Reduced-motion and JavaScript-disabled parity.
- No horizontal overflow.
- Real image alt text and captions.
- Full CI and PDF verification after the design is approved.
