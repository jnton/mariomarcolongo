Public case records

Four different investigation surfaces.

Historical objects, fast-moving health information, research entities and collaborative knowledge bases require different sources. The common discipline is traceability.

01
Historical provenance & rights

Fascist-era carpenter's pencil: provenance, description and permission

Four photographs of a privately held historical carpenter's pencil were supplied directly by the original photographer. The object carries Fascist-era anti-tuberculosis inscriptions and belonged to Aristide Ravaglia, an Italian joiner born in 1890.

Work performed

  • Prepared concise English and Italian descriptions and captions for the front, reverse and angled views.
  • Transcribed and translated the inscriptions while preserving the original political and historical context.
  • Documented ownership context, photographer attribution, source route and reusable licensing information.
  • Published the records through Wikimedia Commons; the visible file page records permission reviewed by Wikimedia's Volunteer Response Team.

Capabilities demonstrated

Source provenanceRights and authorship contextBilingual metadataHistorical object documentation
Evidence boundary

The public record verifies the uploaded media, descriptions, author attribution and VRT permission status. Private correspondence and the complete family-history verification process are not published.

02
Rapid health-information monitoring

H5N1 situation tracker: dated developments and source reconciliation

A public-facing rolling article tracked fast-changing H5N1 developments across human cases, animal outbreaks and public-health reporting.

Work performed

  • Co-authored the tracker and organized developments chronologically so readers could distinguish current information from earlier reporting.
  • Compared reports across scientific, public-health and reputable journalistic sources, preserving outbound citations.
  • Separated confirmed developments from broader pandemic-risk interpretation and updated the article as the evidence changed.
  • Translated a technically complex and uncertain evidence landscape into concise public communication.

Capabilities demonstrated

Live source monitoringChronologyCross-source corroborationUncertainty communication
Evidence boundary

This is a public science-communication record, not an intelligence product or an independent epidemiological surveillance system. Source selection and editorial decisions are visible only to the extent documented in the article.

03
Entity verification & research provenance

Yourself to Science: verifying participation opportunities

A public directory helps people identify research initiatives that accept genomes, health data, biological samples or other forms of participation.

Work performed

  • Defined inclusion and exclusion criteria for clinical studies, biobanks, registries, donation programs and related initiatives.
  • Structured fields for organization, participation type, geographic availability, data or sample type, source URL and licensing context.
  • Reconciled organizations and initiatives across public websites, Wikidata, FAIRsharing, Zenodo and other primary records.
  • Created update and provenance workflows so a visible directory entry can be traced to an inspectable source.

Capabilities demonstrated

Entity reconciliationInclusion criteriaSource traceabilityStructured-data quality
Evidence boundary

The directory verifies public participation information at the time of review. It does not independently audit the scientific quality, privacy practices or institutional claims of every listed initiative.

Long-running public knowledge record

Wikimedia and Wikidata: auditable source and metadata work

One public CentralAuth identity records eight years of activity across English Wikipedia, Italian Wikipedia, Wikidata and Wikimedia Commons.

Work performed

  • Checked citations and public claims, reconciled conflicting source details and improved structured records.
  • Created or published evidence visualizations and open media with source, licensing and descriptive metadata.
  • Worked across prose, structured-data and media repositories rather than a single content format.
  • Maintained a public edit trail that allows individual contributions to be reviewed rather than relying on self-reported totals alone.

Method

A reusable investigation workflow.

The method is intentionally tool-agnostic. Search engines, archives and structured databases change; the reasoning and documentation requirements remain.

01

Question decomposition

Translate a broad claim into identities, dates, locations, source types and falsifiable sub-questions.

02

Source hierarchy

Prioritize primary records, official datasets, scholarly literature and attributable public documentation.

03

Cross-source checking

Compare independent records, dates, wording and metadata; document unresolved disagreement rather than forcing certainty.

04

Provenance & rights

Track who created a record, how it was obtained, what can be published and which licensing or permission evidence exists.

05

Entity reconciliation

Resolve naming variants, organizations, identifiers and relationships across websites and structured databases.

06

Evidence-bound reporting

Separate what the source directly supports from inference, platform labels, private context or unverified interpretation.

Ethical boundary

Open-source research is not permission to expose people.

The portfolio focuses on public-interest knowledge, scientific claims, systems and attributable public records. It does not publish private identifiers, doxxing material, invasive personal profiling or methods designed to bypass legitimate access controls.

For professional trust & safety or investigation work, collection and disclosure should remain proportionate to the risk, authorized by the organization and documented under the applicable privacy and safety rules.
