Testing Approach

The work uses structured, self-directed probing of instruction handling and policy boundaries. The public record supports broad testing categories, but it does not expose enough information to reconstruct every prompt, model output, platform adjudication or model version.

Testing Area 01

Chat and Multi-Turn Instruction Handling

Testing how models preserve instruction hierarchy, contextual constraints and refusal behavior across single-turn and extended dialogue.

Testing Area 02

Multimodal and Vision-Language Inputs

Testing interactions between textual instructions and information embedded in images, diagrams or visual typography.

Testing Area 03

Agentic Tool-Use and Function Selection

Testing whether tool selection and function calls remain aligned with the user’s intended task when external or nested context is present.

Testing Area 04

Indirect Prompt Injection and Untrusted Context

Testing how models handle adversarial instructions found in retrieved documents, web pages or user-provided files.

Limitations and Interpretation

  • The page reports aggregate platform activity and selected public labels, not complete test transcripts.
  • Specific model names, versions and architecture changes are not documented here unless present in the public source.
  • Full prompts, outputs, platform adjudication rationales and exploit payloads are not reproduced.
  • “Platform-confirmed” refers to the Gray Swan platform’s status and is not equivalent to independent third-party reproduction.
  • Rankings and counts are time-sensitive snapshots reported as of July 2026.
  • The record demonstrates sustained evaluation activity; it does not constitute a security certification or a comprehensive audit of any model provider.

Relevance to Evaluation Operations and Research Verification

This work demonstrates structured test design, repeated submission workflows, evidence-bound reporting, classification of observed behaviors, and careful separation between public evidence and interpretation. Those practices are relevant to AI evaluation operations, research verification, quality assurance and technical research roles.

Defensive-design relevance
Adversarial evaluation experience informs threat-modelling and defensive-design considerations for systems that process untrusted inputs.
